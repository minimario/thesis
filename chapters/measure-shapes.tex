\section{Discussion}
In this chapter, we saw several examples of how benchmarks, metrics, and tools shape the
research landscape that follows. In CRUXEval, we introduced code reasoning as an evaluation target,
showing the community that important aspects of coding were worth measuring beyond code generation.
Through LiveCodeBench, we made the idea of time-indexed evaluations more mainstream in the scientific community.
LeanDojo provided an influential benchmark and toolkit that helped researchers advance
the field of theorem proving with language models. ProofOptimizer then illustrated the same
lesson at a smaller scale: once proof length became a measurable target, proof simplification
became something a model could be trained to optimize.

Having seen how what we measure shapes what we build, we close this thesis with a forward-looking report
on the future of software engineering: our perspective on what the field should measure and build next.
Many of its ideas trace directly back to the themes of this thesis. CRUXEval's lesson that generation is only one slice of coding ability
is echoed in our call for measuring capabilities like code optimization and software testing. The contamination and overfitting problems that
motivated LiveCodeBench are fleshed out in Chapter~\ref{chap:position-paper}'s first challenge: evaluation and benchmarks.
Despite being written a year ago in a fast-moving field, most of the challenges we identify remain open today,
which suggests that they are central problems rather than fleeting artifacts.
