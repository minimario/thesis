\subsubsection{Repair}
\label{appendix:repair}
Figures~\ref{fig:repair-full-humaneval}-\ref{fig:repair-full-odex} show the full set of scatterplots for the repair experiments in Sec.~\ref{sec:repair}.
In these plots, \emph{the same model is used for both repair and the initial code generation}, so that the resampling strategy can be represented by the line $y=x$ (simplifying exposition).
Note that even in the most successful setting, DS-I-33b on HumanEval, the number of problems for which repair is more successful than simply resampling is still in the minority (35/81).

Figures~\ref{fig:repair-bars}-\ref{fig:repair-heatmaps} also shows the absolute mean success rate of repair across tasks for each model and dataset, similarly to how was done in the previous sections.
Note that these absolute numbers should not be paid to much attention to, since repair must always be compared to the accuracy of the simple resampling strategy; however, they do show that models do not appear to be better at repairing their own counterfeit samples than those generated by other models.
