\subsection{Discussion}
In this work, we brought attention to the counterfeit samples of a code language model: incorrect
programs that a model thinks are correct and can pass surface-level correctness checks. We observed
that in a sense, these counterfeit samples were adversarial to the model: models often could not assess
their correctness, reason about their execution, and struggled to repair them. One important takeaway
is that correct and counterfeit samples paint very different pictures when it comes to evaluating
model capabilities. For example, we saw that models were much better at assessing the
correctness of correct samples compared to counterfeit samples. Focusing on counterfeit samples painted
a different picture of model capabilities, showing that models had significant gaps in their understanding of code semantics.
