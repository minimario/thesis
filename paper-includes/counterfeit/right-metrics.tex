\subsection{Discussion}
In this work, we bring attention to the counterfeit samples of a code language model: incorrect
programs that a model thinks are correct and can pass surface-level correctness checks. We observe
that in a sense, these counterfeit samples are adversarial to the model: models often cannot assess
their correctness, reason about their execution, and struggle to repair them. One important takeaway
is that correct and counterfeit samples paint very different pictures when it comes to evaluating
model capabilities. For example, we saw that models are much better at assessing the 
correctness of correct samples compared to counterfeit samples. Focusing on counterfeit samples paints 
a different picture of model capabilities, showing that models have significant gaps in their understanding of code semantics.
