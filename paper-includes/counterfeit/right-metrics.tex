\subsection{Discussion}
In this work, we brought attention to the counterfeit samples of a code language model: incorrect
programs that a model thinks are correct and can pass surface-level correctness checks. While previous
works had suggested that code language models could understand and self-correct code, we saw that
this was partially because many of the incorrect programs they were evaluating were likely
blatantly incorrect programs. In contrast, by focusing on counterfeit samples, we saw a very different 
picture: models struggled to assess correctness, reason about execution, and repair these programs. 
Again, we see that eliminating confounding factors is critical in developing a complete understanding
of a model's capabilities.
