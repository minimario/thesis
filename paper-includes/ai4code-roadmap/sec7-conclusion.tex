\subsection{Discussion}
In this chapter, we have briefly situated several task families in AI for software engineering and focused on formal verification as a representative task. We have also highlighted critical cross-cutting challenges that permeate throughout many software-engineering tasks. Finally, to drive progress in AI for code, we've pinpointed a set of promising research directions for alleviating these challenges and advancing AI towards being a more capable software engineer.

In many ways, this chapter reflects the lessons developed throughout the thesis. 
In Chapter 2, we saw how each metric revealed a different slice of AI systems and showed different insights. 
The formal-verification discussion in this chapter continues that line of thinking, but adapts it to the capabilities and ambitions of today's language models.
Chapter 3 then showed the importance of deeper analysis, which motivated several of the challenges in this chapter: the need to isolate capabilities, 
avoid benchmark-specific overfitting, and semantically understand codebases. 
Finally, Chapter 4 showed how evaluations can shape the direction of future research and science. 
For example, CRUXEval did not only reveal a missing capability; it helped make execution reasoning a target for later benchmarks and training methods. 
The paths forward in this chapter follow from that perspective. They ask what kinds of data, environments, retrieval methods, and tools would encourage the next generation of coding systems to develop the capabilities that real software engineering requires.
